What is Origami?

Origami is the traditional Japanese art or technique of folding paper into a variety of decorative or representational forms, of animals or flowers, etc.



History of Origami

By the 7th century, paper had been introduced to Japan from China via the Korean Peninsula, and the Japanese developed washi by improving the method of making paper in the Heian period.

The paper making technique developed in Japan around 805 to 809 was called nagashi-suki (流し漉き), a method of adding mucilage to the process of the conventional tame-suki (溜め漉き) technique to form a stronger layer of paper fibers.
